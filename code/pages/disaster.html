Disaster Preparedness

Planning ahead reduces stress and risk when storms, fires, or power outages strike. Take these steps now:

  • Build an Emergency Kit: Include 3 days’ worth of non-perishable food, 1 gallon of water per person per day, flashlight, batteries, and first-aid supplies.
  • Draft an Evacuation Plan: Map two escape routes from home and work, choose a rendezvous point, and practice drills twice a year.
  • Set Up a Communication Plan: Designate an out-of-area contact, keep phones charged with power banks, and agree on a simple “I’m safe” text.
  • Secure Your Home: Anchor heavy furniture, install smoke/CO detectors, and learn how to turn off gas, water, and electricity.

Review and refresh your kit and plan each spring and fall to stay ready year-round.
