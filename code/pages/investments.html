Investment Strategies

Once you’ve established your savings, investing helps your money keep pace with inflation—and ideally grow faster. Smart diversification and cost control are key.

  • Define Your Risk Profile: Younger retirees may tolerate more market swings for higher returns; those closer to retirement often shift to bonds or stable-value funds to preserve capital.
  • Diversify Your Portfolio: Spread your investments across stocks, bonds, and real-estate funds to reduce the impact of any single market downturn and smooth overall returns.
  • Keep Costs Low: Choose low-fee index funds or ETFs over high-expense-ratio mutual funds. Fees of even 1% can shave hundreds of thousands off your balance over decades.
